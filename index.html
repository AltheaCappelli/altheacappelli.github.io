Hi, I'm Althea, and welcome to my website!

Here I share coding projects I’ve been working on in my free time out of curiosity.
I studied physics at Leibniz University Hannover, where I focused on theoretical physics during my Master’s and spent a semester abroad in Helsinki. I’m passionate about coding, solving complex problems, and contributing to a more sustainable future.
In my Bachelor’s thesis, I explored the probability of recovering quantum information from a black hole using tensor networks. For my Master’s, I modeled a proposed dark matter detection experiment and simulated its response to a theoretical signal using numerical methods.
Alongside my studies, I took extra lectures in computer physics and data science in Python, and have since deepened my skills in data analysis and machine learning. I also picked up HTML, CSS, and SQL to build this website and support my projects.
Until recently, I worked as a lab assistant, helping students with experiments and Python-based data analysis. Before that, I taught theoretical physics exercises for several years.
I speak Italian, German, and English fluently, plus Spanish and a bit of Finnish. Outside of work, you’ll likely find me bouldering, training taekwondo, or out hiking somewhere in nature :)
